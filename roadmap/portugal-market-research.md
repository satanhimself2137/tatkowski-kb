## Findings

### 1. Language Pairs — Demand Ranking (Portugal, 2025)

Based on AIMA 2024 migration data: 1,543,697 foreign residents at end of 2024 (up from 421,802 in 2017).

**Tier 1 — High volume, immediate commercial demand:**

| Language | Community size | Key need |
|---|---|---|
| Ukrainian ↔ English/Portuguese | ~56,700 under temp protection (Feb 2025) | Medical appointments, employment, legal, AIMA proceedings |
| Hindi / Gujarati / Punjabi ↔ English/Portuguese | ~104,000–120,000 Indians (2nd largest group, 7.4%) | Employment contracts, occupational health exams, HR |
| Nepali ↔ English/Portuguese | 9,411 first permits 2024 (4th largest) | Agriculture, construction, hospitality — fast-growing, underserved |
| English ↔ Portuguese | Baseline — expats, B2B, legal, real estate | Consistent across all sectors |

**Tier 2 — Steady demand, competitive:**

| Language | Driver |
|---|---|
| French ↔ Portuguese | CPLP Africa (Angola 6.9%, Cape Verde), Francophone expats |
| Arabic ↔ Portuguese | Asylum seekers (Senegal, Gambia top 2024 nationalities); Moroccan/Algerian workers |
| Spanish ↔ Portuguese | Latin American business, Galician border trade |

**Tier 3 — Niche, premium rates:**

- Konkani / Gujarati for Goan-heritage community (Lisbon, Faro, Beja districts)
- German ↔ Portuguese (tech sector, German expat retirees Algarve)

**Key insight:** Brazilian Portuguese (31.4% of all foreign residents) does NOT generate interpreting demand — they speak Portuguese. They DO generate document certification demand: Brazilian PT documents often need European PT re-certification for AIMA, university recognition, and employment. This is a translation (certified) play, not interpreting.

### 2. B2B Sectors That Purchase Interpreting

**Sector 1: Occupational Health (Medicina do Trabalho) — HIGHEST PRIORITY**

Portuguese law mandates every employer to contract an occupational health provider for periodic medical fitness exams. With 1.5M+ foreign workers, many exams need interpreters. Direct analogue to Medmark/Fyffes model in Ireland.

Key providers to pitch (they supply services to employers with foreign workers):
- **SEPRI** — sepri.pt — occupational medicine and workplace health, multiple PT locations
- Regional Clínicas de Medicina do Trabalho (search by district via ACT portal)
- **ACT (Autoridade para as Condições de Trabalho)** — the national regulator; can provide clinic lists

### 3. Regulatory Demand Drivers

- **Medicina do Trabalho (Law 102/2009):** Mandatory occupational health exams for all workers. Non-Portuguese-speaking workers require interpreter. This is a compliance-driven, recurring need — not discretionary.
- **AIMA proceedings:** 213,000+ appointments in 2024. Government uses multilingual sociocultural mediators in-house, but private cases (law firm clients) need their own interpreter.
- **Court interpreting:** Criminal and civil proceedings involving non-PT speakers require certified court interpreters. Referral source: criminal defence lawyers.
