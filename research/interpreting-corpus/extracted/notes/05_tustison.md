## Strategic framing (Tustison's intro)
- "The single biggest challenge for freelancers is finding clients."
- Agencies exist as the middleman precisely because freelancers can't find clients efficiently on their own. Two honest reasons agencies are valuable: they're positioned to find clients, and they want to make money.
- Flip for Tatkowski: **we are the agency holding the middleman position**, so finding clients well IS our core value-add; the contractor relationships are our supply side, not our customers.
- Application discipline (his advice to freelancers, useful for our outbound to peer agencies): ~30 minutes/day, 2–3×/week minimum, research each target, customise, repeat. Doing nothing guarantees nothing.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR TATKOWSKI

1. **Reciprocal-referral network.** Most listed agencies don't cover our specific IE+UK+ES+PT spread under one roof. Pitch a reciprocal arrangement: we're their IE/UK/ES/PT certified-translation + court/medical-interpreting subcontractor; they refer in-market work to us, we refer their language pairs out to them. Target ~10–20 partnerships for surge capacity + a referral pipeline.
2. **"Interpreting-focused" is rare.** The directory is overwhelmingly translation shops; explicit interpreting focus (e.g. A To Z) is uncommon — our interpreting-lead positioning is a genuine differentiator.
3. **David — Portugal list.** Use the verified PT agencies for (a) reciprocity pitches, (b) sourcing freelance translator/interpreter contractors, (c) spotting B2B end-clients that might come to us directly.
4. **Magda — Ireland list.** Use for reciprocal partnerships (Vistatec is the big name worth knowing), competitor positioning research, and market awareness for outreach.
