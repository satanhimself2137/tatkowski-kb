# Interpreting Corpus — Reading List

Curated for the literature-derived market intelligence project. Each entry is grep-friendly: title, author, edition, ISBN where known, one-line relevance, status.

Tiers reflect priority for extraction, not literary quality. Tier 1 = direct business operations data. Tier 2 = setting-specific job mechanics. Tier 3 = cross-national ethnographic datasets. Tier 4 = case-driven industry accounts.

---

## Tier 1 — Direct business operations

**Getting Started as a Freelance Interpreter**
Author: Corinne McKay
Edition: 1st (2024/25)
ISBN: 979-8218429898 (paperback)
Publisher: Two Rat Press
Relevance: Current practitioner standard. Certification paths, agency rosters, court rosters, direct clients. Explicit chapters on finding work and pricing.
Status: pending purchase

**How to Succeed as a Freelance Translator**
Author: Corinne McKay
Edition: 3rd
ISBN: 978-1532788383
Publisher: Two Rat Press
Relevance: Sister volume for translation. Pricing, client acquisition, agency vs direct.
Status: pending purchase

**The Entrepreneurial Linguist: The Business-School Approach to Freelance Translation**
Authors: Judy Jenner, Dagmar Jenner
Edition: 1st (2010)
ISBN: 978-0557256235
Publisher: EL Press
Relevance: Court-certified interpreter + MBA. Direct client acquisition, marketing, positioning against low-cost agencies. Social media chapters dated; ignore.
Status: pending purchase

**A Practical Guide for Translators**
Author: Geoffrey Samuelsson-Brown
Edition: 5th (2010)
ISBN: 978-1847692597
Publisher: Multilingual Matters
Relevance: Author ran an agency, not just freelance. Pricing, dispute resolution, copyright, client payment failure. Closest analogue to our operating model.
Status: pending purchase

**The Business Guide for Translators** (formerly Business School for Translators material)
Author: Marta Stelmaszak
Edition: 1st (2015)
ISBN: 978-1505787900
Publisher: self-published
Relevance: SWOT, PESTEL, Porter's five forces, Blue Ocean applied to language services.
Status: availability problem — often out of print. Try secondhand or contact author direct.

## Tier 2 — Domain-specific practitioner texts

**Introduction to Court Interpreting**
Author: Holly Mikkelson
Edition: 2nd (2017)
ISBN: 978-1138916487
Publisher: Routledge (Translation Practices Explained series)
Relevance: Court standard. Criminal/civil procedure flow, who books, jurisdictional differences. Postville ICE raid section.
Status: pending purchase

**Introduction to Healthcare for Interpreters and Translators**
Author: Ineke H.M. Crezee
Edition: 1st (2013)
ISBN: 978-9027212047
Publisher: John Benjamins
Relevance: Hospital workflows ER through discharge, by specialty. Maps medical interpreting demand to clinical event types.
Status: pending purchase

**The Medical Interpreter: A Foundation Textbook for Medical Interpreting**
Authors: Marjory Bancroft, Sofia García-Beyaert, Katharine Allen, Giovanna Carriero-Contreras, Denis Socarrás-Estrada
Edition: 1st (2016)
ISBN: 978-0997402810
Publisher: Cross Cultural Communications
Relevance: US-leaning but covers language access law, who pays (insurance, hospital, state), how referrals route through hospital admin.
Status: pending purchase

**The Community Interpreter: An International Textbook**
Authors: Marjory Bancroft, Sofia García-Beyaert, Katharine Allen, Giovanna Carriero-Contreras, Denis Socarrás-Estrada
Edition: 1st (2015)
ISBN: 978-0997402803
Publisher: Cross Cultural Communications
Relevance: Companion volume covering social services, schools, asylum, NGO settings — where many of our community jobs originate.
Status: pending purchase

**Conference Interpreting: A Trainer's Guide**
Authors: Robin Setton, Andrew Dawrant
Edition: 1st (2016)
ISBN: 978-9027258625
Publisher: John Benjamins
Relevance: Less interesting for skills, very interesting for market structure — chief interpreters, recruiters, day-rate tiers, AIIC chambers.
Status: pending purchase

## Tier 3 — Industry ethnography

**The Routledge Handbook of Public Service Interpreting**
Editors: Laura Gavioli, Cecilia Wadensjö
Edition: 1st (2023)
ISBN: 978-0367278427 (hbk) / 978-1032391151 (pbk) / 978-0429298202 (ebk)
Publisher: Routledge
Relevance: ~450 pages, 25+ chapters across asylum, court, medical, social services, domestic violence. Cross-national. The big one.
Status: pending purchase — pricey, €60+ paperback

**Intercultural Mediation in Healthcare: From the Professional Medical Interpreters' Perspective**
Author: Izabel E.T. de V. Souza
Edition: 1st (2016)
ISBN: 978-1524512729
Publisher: AuthorHouse
Relevance: Doctoral study, 458 interpreters across 25 countries. Rare cross-national quantitative dataset; essentially re-tabulatable.
Status: pending purchase

## Tier 4 — Industry case-driven accounts

**Found in Translation: How Language Shapes Our Lives and Transforms the World**
Authors: Nataly Kelly, Jost Zetzsche
Edition: 1st (2012)
ISBN: 978-0399537974
Publisher: Perigee Trade / Penguin
Relevance: Both deep industry insiders (ex-CSA Research, Tool Box journal). Case-driven, commercially literate, light on romance.
Status: pending purchase

**Interpreting after the Largest ICE Raid in US History: A Personal Account**
Author: Erik Camayd-Freixas
Edition: essay/monograph (2008 essay; expanded later)
Publisher: ATA / Florida International University
Relevance: One case, deeply documented — how 300+ simultaneous interpreting needs got sourced, what went wrong, how the agency layer behaved under pressure. Forensic detail the schema needs.
Status: pending — essay freely available online; expanded book version harder to find

## Industry data (not books — separate budget line)

**Slator Pro Guides** — annual reports on LSP market segments. Paid subscription ~€400/yr. https://slator.com
**Nimdzi Insights** — Nimdzi 100 ranking and segment reports. Mixed free/paid. https://www.nimdzi.com
**CSA Research** (Common Sense Advisory) — historical industry research; subscription-based. https://csa-research.com

Decision: hold these until corpus extraction is proven on books. If extraction yields usable schema data, then add one year of Slator Pro as the quantitative anchor.

## Online discovery / lookup directories

For finding, comparing editions, and locating copies:

- **WorldCat** — https://www.worldcat.org — global library catalogue, 10,000+ libraries. Best for finding an academic book in a library near Dublin without buying.
- **Open Library** — https://openlibrary.org — Internet Archive's catalogue; some titles borrowable as scanned ebooks for free.
- **Google Books** — https://books.google.com — preview pages, sometimes full text for older works.
- **Goodreads** — https://www.goodreads.com — reviews + edition tracking; useful for spotting which edition is the current one.
- **AbeBooks** — https://www.abebooks.com — secondhand worldwide; cheapest route for out-of-print titles (Stelmaszak in particular).
- **John Benjamins catalogue** — https://benjamins.com — direct from publisher for Benjamins titles (Crezee, Setton/Dawrant, Wadensjö volumes); often cheaper than Amazon for academic translation studies.
- **Routledge** — https://www.routledge.com — direct from publisher for the Routledge handbooks; watch for periodic 30% sales.
