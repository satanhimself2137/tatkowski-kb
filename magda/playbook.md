# Magda — IE B2B Outreach Playbook

Internal working doc maintained by Claude on Magda's behalf. Plain-language, no jargon.

---

## What we sell (one paragraph she can copy into any first message)

Tatkowski Interpreting & Recruitment is an Irish-registered certified translation and interpreting agency. Certified translations accepted by INIS/ISD, courts, registrars and universities. On-site, phone and video interpreting (Ukrainian, Russian, Polish, Portuguese, Arabic, Lithuanian, Mandarin and more), with 24-hour turnaround on most documents. One supplier for both translation and interpreting — quoted as totals, delivered on time, certification stands behind every document.

## Sectors we target in Ireland (priority order)

1. **Immigration lawyers** — biggest recurring need. Certified document translations for visa applications, ISD appeals, citizenship applications; interpreters for client meetings and tribunal hearings. Dublin first, then Cork and Galway.
2. **Notaries** — apostille-paired translations, deeds for non-English-speaking clients. Dublin commuter belt where there's high non-Irish population.
3. **Private healthcare** — Beacon, Blackrock Health, Bons Secours, Hermitage, Mater Private. Interpreters for consultations with non-English-speaking patients. Compliance angle (informed consent must be in patient's language).
4. **Corporate HR** — international employers with non-Irish workforces. Pharma (Pfizer, MSD), tech (Google, Meta, LinkedIn — Dublin), construction (Sisk, BAM, John Sisk & Son), manufacturing. Interpreters for HR meetings, occupational health, contract signing.
5. **Visa consultancies / immigration advice services** — recurring document-translation contracts.

## Tone and channels

- **LinkedIn DM** — most reliable B2B channel for first contact. Find the named person (HR Director, Operations Manager, Office Manager at a law firm) rather than messaging the company page.
- **Email** — second contact or follow-up. Always sent from contact@tatkowski.com.
- **Walk-ins** — only when nearby and a known prospect. Bring a one-pager. Magda does these only if comfortable; not required.
- **Tone** — warm, professional, no hard sell. We help with a problem they already have. We are not "the cheapest option" — we're the one that gets it right first time and stands behind the certification.

## What never to do without checking

- **Never quote a custom rate** without Claude or Maciej confirming. Standard certified translation is €39.99/page, but B2B contracts and bulk work get bespoke rates — wait for sign-off.
- **Never promise turnaround** outside the standard windows. If a prospect wants 24h on a 15-page document, flag it.
- **Never sign a contract or agreement.** Forward anything contractual to Maciej.
- **Never share interpreter direct contact details** with a client before payment.

## What to do when someone replies

1. Tell Claude in your chat — "X from Y replied, here's what they said."
2. Claude updates `magda/ie-prospects.md` with the new stage and next action.
3. Claude drafts the next message. Magda reviews and sends.
4. If the reply needs Maciej (price negotiation, contract talk, edge case), Claude flags him.

## Weekly rhythm (suggested)

- **Monday:** Claude pulls 5–10 new prospects with named contacts. Magda reviews the list.
- **Tuesday–Wednesday:** First-touch DMs and emails go out.
- **Thursday:** Follow-ups on Monday's silent contacts.
- **Friday:** Review the week with Claude. What got replies, what didn't, what to try differently next week.

## What "won" looks like

A first paid interpreting job from a new B2B client is a win. Even a small one — a single one-hour Mandarin interpreting session for a private clinic — opens the door for the recurring relationship. The €5–10k/month target is built from 8–12 active B2B clients each booking 2–6 hours/month at €120–€150/hr first-hour rates.
